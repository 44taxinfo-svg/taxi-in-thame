Which Airport is Best from Thame?
Heathrow, Luton, Gatwick & Stansted Compared

A practical comparison from a driver's perspective — helping you choose the right airport for your trip.

📅 January 2025 ⏱️ 6 min read 📝 O2Taxi

One of the most common questions we get from passengers in Thame and South Oxfordshire is: "Which airport should I use?" With four major airports within taxi range — Heathrow, Luton, Gatwick and Stansted — the answer depends on a few factors: your destination, your budget and how early you need to leave.

Here's an honest breakdown from the people who make the journey every day.

Airport Comparison from Thame

✈️ Heathrow Airport (LHR)

📍 Distance: ~43 miles ⏱️ Journey time: 50–65 min 💷 O2Taxi from: £75

Heathrow is the UK's biggest and busiest airport, and the most popular choice from Thame by far. The route via the A40 or M40/M25 is straightforward and well-maintained. Heathrow offers the most destinations of any UK airport — especially for long-haul — and has excellent facilities across all terminals.

  • Best for: Long-haul flights, business travel, wide choice of airlines
  • Terminals: T2, T3, T4, T5 (know yours before you travel)
  • Watch out for: Morning traffic on the M25 approaching T4 and T5
✅ Our verdict: Best all-round choice from Thame for most travellers.

✈️ Luton Airport (LTN)

📍 Distance: ~38 miles ⏱️ Journey time: 45–55 min 💷 O2Taxi from: £70

Luton is the closest airport to Thame and often the most affordable option. It's the home base for easyJet and operates a good range of European routes. The route via the A418 and A505 is mostly dual carriageway and avoids motorways almost entirely.

  • Best for: Budget airlines, European breaks, quick trips
  • Airlines: easyJet, Wizz Air, Ryanair, TUI
  • Watch out for: Luton is a smaller airport — fewer premium facilities
✅ Our verdict: Best value from Thame. Ideal for budget European travel.

✈️ Gatwick Airport (LGW)

📍 Distance: ~88 miles ⏱️ Journey time: 85–100 min 💷 O2Taxi from: £110

Gatwick is the furthest of the four airports from Thame, but it's a strong option if your airline only flies from there. The route down the M40 and M25 is reliable, though it's a longer commitment. Gatwick has two terminals — North and South — so confirm which you need.

  • Best for: Routes not available from closer airports
  • Airlines: easyJet (South), British Airways, Norwegian, TUI
  • Watch out for: The longer journey means an earlier start
⚠️ Our verdict: Worth it only if your route is Gatwick-exclusive.

✈️ Stansted Airport (STN)

📍 Distance: ~88 miles ⏱️ Journey time: 90–105 min 💷 O2Taxi from: £110

Stansted is the UK's third-largest airport and the main hub for Ryanair. It's a similar distance from Thame to Gatwick, accessed via the M40 and M11. Stansted has just one terminal, which keeps things simple once you arrive.

  • Best for: Ryanair routes to Europe and beyond
  • Airlines: Ryanair, easyJet, Jet2, Wizz Air
  • Watch out for: Long journey — plan for an early pickup
⚠️ Our verdict: A good option if Ryanair has the best deal on your route.

Our Recommendation

If you have flexibility, Heathrow is usually the best choice from Thame — it's well-connected, not too far, and serves virtually every destination worldwide. For budget European flights, Luton is the most practical option given its proximity and competitive fares.
